## The Brittleness Problem Nobody Talks About

## Why Traditional Solutions Don't Work Anymore

For decades, B2B SaaS companies had two options when customers demanded customization.

### Option 1: Engineering Roadmaps

You prioritize features. Engineering builds them. Customers wait.

The problem? It's too slow and too expensive.

One founder I spoke with runs an 11-year-old SaaS platform with hundreds of customers. He told me: "We've got a lot of features, and there's a lot there. But there's never enough. There's always stuff we can't get to."

The math doesn't work. You have limited engineering resources. Every persona wants different things. Even if you could build everything, you'd spend years catching up to feature requests while new ones pile up faster than you can ship.

And here's the worst part: half the features you do build turn out to be wrong. You thought customers needed X, but they actually needed Y. By the time you realize it, you've burned months of engineering time.

### Option 2: Implementation Services

Some SaaS companies solve this by offering custom implementation. You send a team to build exactly what each customer needs.

Great for the customer. Terrible for your business.

You just converted your product company into a service agency. Your margins collapse. Each customer's implementation is different. You can't productize what you learn. Maintenance becomes a nightmare.

This is why consultants charge hundreds of thousands of dollars to implement enterprise software. It's the only way the economics work. And it only works because customers historically had no alternative.

They don't anymore.
